# Sick Leave Policy

Employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per year. Sick leave may be used for personal illness, injury, or medical appointments.

If an employee is sick for more than 2 consecutive days, a medical certificate may be required.

Unused sick leave cannot be carried forward to the next year.


# Work From Office Policy

Employees are expected to work from the office on designated in-office days as determined by their department.

Employees must badge in and out using the attendance system.

Repeated failure to comply with in-office requirements may result in corrective action.

# Leave of Absence Policy

Employees may request unpaid leave for personal or family reasons.

Leave requests must be submitted at least 30 days in advance whenever possible.

Approval is subject to business needs and management discretion.


# Dress Code Policy

Employees are expected to maintain a professional and neat appearance while at work.

Clothing that is offensive, unsafe, or inappropriate for the workplace is not permitted.

Departments may specify additional dress requirements based on job duties.


# IT and Acceptable Use Policy

Company computers, email accounts, and internet access are provided for business purposes.

Employees must not install unauthorized software or access harmful websites.

All digital activities may be monitored to ensure security and compliance.


# Data Privacy Policy

Employees must handle personal and company data responsibly and in accordance with privacy laws.

Sensitive information must not be shared without authorization.

Data breaches must be reported immediately to the IT department.


# Workplace Safety Policy

Employees must follow all safety guidelines and use protective equipment where required.

Hazards or unsafe conditions must be reported immediately.

Failure to comply with safety procedures may result in disciplinary action.


# Anti-Harassment Policy

The company maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward harassment or bullying of any kind.

Employees who experience or witness harassment should report it to HR immediately.

All complaints will be investigated confidentially.


# Training and Development Policy

Employees are encouraged to participate in training programs to enhance their skills.

The company may sponsor approved professional development courses.

Attendance at mandatory training sessions is required.


# Performance Evaluation Policy

Employee performance reviews will be conducted annually.

Feedback will be provided on goals, strengths, and areas for improvement.

Performance results may influence promotions, bonuses, or compensation adjustments.


# Termination Policy

Employment may be terminated voluntarily or involuntarily according to company guidelines.

Employees are expected to provide notice before resignation.

Final settlements and company property return must be completed on the last working day.


# Holiday Policy

Employees are entitled to company-declared public holidays each year.

If business needs require working on a holiday, compensatory leave or pay may be provided.

Holiday schedules will be announced at the beginning of each year.


# Travel Policy

All official travel must be approved by the manager prior to booking.

Employees should use company-approved travel vendors.

Travel expenses must follow reimbursement guidelines and budget limits.


# Employee Grievance Policy

Employees may raise workplace concerns without fear of retaliation.

Grievances should first be discussed with the immediate manager or HR.

The company will investigate concerns promptly and fairly.
